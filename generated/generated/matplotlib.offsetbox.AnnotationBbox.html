30.2.3. matplotlib.offsetbox.AnnotationBbox¶

class matplotlib.offsetbox.AnnotationBbox(offsetbox, xy, xybox=None, xycoords=u'data', boxcoords=None, frameon=True, pad=0.4, annotation_clip=None, box_alignment=(0.5, 0.5), bboxprops=None, arrowprops=None, fontsize=None, **kwargs)[source]¶

Annotation-like class, but with offsetbox instead of Text.

__init__(offsetbox, xy, xybox=None, xycoords=u'data', boxcoords=None, frameon=True, pad=0.4, annotation_clip=None, box_alignment=(0.5, 0.5), bboxprops=None, arrowprops=None, fontsize=None, **kwargs)[source]¶

offsetbox : OffsetBox instance

xycoords
: same as Annotation but can be a tuple of two
strings which are interpreted as x and y coordinates.
boxcoords
: similar to textcoords as Annotation but can be a
tuple of two strings which are interpreted as x and y coordinates.
box_alignment
: a tuple of two floats for a vertical and
horizontal alignment of the offset box w.r.t. the boxcoords. The lower-left corner is (0.0) and upper-right corner is (1.1).

other parameters are identical to that of Annotation.
